\section*{Proof — RA-ABB-C01-S1.3-E03B}

\noindent
\hyperlink{ws-RA-ABB-C01-S1.3-E03B}{\textbf{← Back to worksheet}}

\noindent
\textbf{Source.}
\srccite{AbbottUnderstandingAnalysis}{Chapter~1, \S1.3, Exercise~1.3.3(b)}.

\vspace{0.75em}

\noindent
\textbf{Goal.} Use part (a) to explain why there is no need to assert that greatest lower bounds exist as part of the Axiom of Completeness.

\vspace{0.75em}

\noindent
\textbf{Proof (stub).}
\begin{itemize}
\item State the Axiom of Completeness in the “least upper bound” form (LUB property).
\item Starting from a nonempty set $A$ bounded below, define $B$ as the set of lower bounds of $A$.
\item Use LUB on $B$ to \emph{produce} $\sup B$, and invoke (a) to identify $\sup B=\inf A$.
\item Conclude: existence of infimum is derivable from existence of supremum.
\end{itemize}
