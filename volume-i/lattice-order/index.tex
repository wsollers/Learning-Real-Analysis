\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What this chapter is for.}
The order chapter in Sets, Relations, and Functions already introduces
partially ordered sets, suprema and infima, and the first appearance of
lattices. This chapter is the natural future home for the subject once we want
to treat lattice order as a topic in its own right rather than as one stop in
the general theory of relations and order.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What the breadcrumb points toward.}
Lattice theory sits at a crossroads. It connects order, algebra, topology,
Boolean algebras, sigma-algebras, fixed-point theorems, domain theory, and
later categorical viewpoints. So this chapter is a marker: the terrain has
appeared, and a fuller development belongs here.
