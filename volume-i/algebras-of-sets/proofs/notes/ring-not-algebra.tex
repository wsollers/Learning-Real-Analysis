\FlashProof{Let $X=\mathbb{N}$ and $\mathcal{R}=\{A\subseteq\mathbb{N}:A\text{ finite}\}$. Ring: (R1) $\emptyset$ finite; (R2) finite union of finite sets is finite; (R3) subset of finite set is finite. Not an algebra: $\{0\}\in\mathcal{R}$ but $\{0\}^c=\mathbb{N}\setminus\{0\}$ is infinite, so $\{0\}^c\notin\mathcal{R}$, violating (A2).}
\end{remark*}
