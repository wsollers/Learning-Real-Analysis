\FlashProof{Let $X=\{1,2,3\}$, $\mathcal{F}_1=\{\emptyset,\{1\},\{2,3\},X\}$, $\mathcal{F}_2=\{\emptyset,\{2\},\{1,3\},X\}$. Each is a $\sigma$-algebra (verify the three axioms). In $\mathcal{F}_1\cup\mathcal{F}_2$ we have $\{1\}$ and $\{2\}$, but $\{1\}\cup\{2\}=\{1,2\}\notin\mathcal{F}_1\cup\mathcal{F}_2$. So $\mathcal{F}_1\cup\mathcal{F}_2$ fails ($\sigma$3).}
