\FlashProof{Let $\mathcal{S} = \sigma(\{\{x\}:x\in X\})$. ($\subseteq$): $\mathcal{S}\subseteq\mathcal{P}(X)$ trivially. ($\supseteq$): Let $A\subseteq X$. Since $X$ is countable, $A$ is countable, so $A = \bigcup_{x\in A}\{x\}$ is a countable union of singletons. Each $\{x\}\in\mathcal{S}$ by construction, so $A\in\mathcal{S}$ by ($\sigma$3).}
