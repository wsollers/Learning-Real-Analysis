▸ Classical differential geometry — the concrete geometric-analytic combination
Differential Geometry of Curves and Surfaces
do Carmo · 1976
start here — curves & surfaces in ℝ³, deeply analytic
Begin do Carmo when multivariable analysis is solid. The first chapter revisits parametric curves from Calc 3 but with geometric depth. Curvature, Gauss-Bonnet, and the shape operator will feel like the "why" behind everything from engineering math.

Ideals, Varieties, and Algorithms
Cox, Little & O'Shea · 4th ed.
start here — accessible, computational, builds from scratch
Cox-Little-O'Shea requires only linear algebra and some ring theory — it develops Gröbner bases and variety theory algorithmically. Your software background will make this feel natural. An ideal bridge from abstract algebra into geometry.

An Introduction to Manifolds
Tu · 2nd ed.
smooth manifolds, differential forms, de Rham cohomology
After do Carmo you will have seen curvature and surfaces concretely. Tu abstracts this: manifolds, tangent spaces, differential forms. The language here unlocks everything that follows.

▸ Where algebra and complex analysis converge on curves
Algebraic Curves and Riemann Surfaces for Undergraduates
Nerode & Greenberg · 2022
the destination — theory of the donut
By the time you arrive here, you will have the algebraic geometry from phases 3–5 and the analytic intuition from do Carmo and Tu. Riemann surfaces will feel like the natural meeting point of everything — not a mountain to climb, but a view you've been ascending toward.

complex analysis to be added between phases 4 and 6 — Needham's Visual Complex Analysis recommended
