\subsection*{Divisibility Bound}
\label{prf:div-bound}

\begin{remark*}[Return]
\hyperref[lem:div-bound]{$\leftarrow$ Back to Lemma (Divisibility Bound) in Notes}
\end{remark*}

\begin{proof}
\Claim If $b \mid a$ and $a \neq 0$, then $|b| \leq |a|$.

\Given $b \mid a$ and $a \neq 0$.
\Goal To show $|b| \leq |a|$.

\ByDef{Divisibility}, $b \mid a$ means there exists an integer $c$ such that
\[
  a = b \cdot c.
\]

Taking absolute values,
\[
  |a| = |b \cdot c| = |b||c|.
\]

\ByAss $a \neq 0$, so both $b$ and $c$ cannot equal zero
(since $b = 0$ or $c = 0$ would force $a = b \cdot c = 0$, contradicting the hypothesis).
\Therefore $|c| \geq 1$.

\Hence
\[
  |a| = |b||c| \geq |b| \cdot 1 = |b|,
\]
that is, $|b| \leq |a|$. \AsReq
\end{proof}

\begin{remark*}[Proof shape]
Direct proof. Unpack the definition of divisibility to get $a = bc$, then
take absolute values. The hypothesis $a \neq 0$ is used to guarantee $|c| \geq 1$
via the zero product property, which turns $|a| = |b||c|$ into the bound $|b| \leq |a|$.
