\textbf{Goal.}
Suppose $a \in \mathbb{F}$, $v \in V$, and $av = \mathbf{0}$.
Prove that $a = 0$ or $v = \mathbf{0}$.

\vspace{0.75em}
\noindent
\begin{proof}
Suppose $av = \mathbf{0}$. We proceed by cases.

Case 1. $a = 0$

0v = 0

Case 2. $a != 0$

av = 0
$a^-1av = a^-10$

$v = 0$


\end{proof}
