\begin{proof}
Let $R$ be an integral domain, $a, b, c \in R$, and $a \neq 0$.
Assume $ab = ac$.

\noindent
\begin{tabular}{T S J}
\toprule
\textbf{Tag} & \multicolumn{1}{p{0.44\textwidth}}{\textbf{Step}} & \textbf{Justification} \\
\midrule
\addlinespace[4pt]

\tagAM
&
ab - ac = 0
&
Subtract $ac$ from both sides of $ab = ac$.
\\[10pt]

\tagDU
&
a(b - c) = 0
&
Left distributivity (Axiom R3): $ab - ac = a(b-c)$.
\\[10pt]

\tagDU
&
a = 0 \;\text{ or }\; b - c = 0
&
$R$ is an integral domain: $a(b-c) = 0$ implies
$a = 0$ or $b - c = 0$.
\\[10pt]

\tagAM
&
b - c = 0
&
We assumed $a \neq 0$, so $b - c = 0$.
\\[10pt]

\tagAM
&
b = c
&
Adding $c$ to both sides of $b - c = 0$.
\hfill$\blacksquare$
\\[6pt]

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{proof}
