\begin{theorem*}[Monotonicity of the Supremum]
Let $A,B \subseteq \mathbb{R}$ be nonempty and bounded above.  
If $A \subseteq B$, then
\[
\sup A \le \sup B .
\]
\end{theorem*}

\begin{proof}
Since $B$ is bounded above, $\sup B$ exists and is an upper bound of $B$.  
Thus
\[
b \le \sup B \quad \text{for all } b \in B .
\]

Because $A \subseteq B$, every element of $A$ is also an element of $B$.  
Hence
\[
a \le \sup B \quad \text{for all } a \in A .
\]

Therefore $\sup B$ is an upper bound of $A$.

Since $\sup A$ is the least upper bound of $A$, it follows that
\[
\sup A \le \sup B .
\]
\end{proof}
