\begin{theorem*}
Let $A \subseteq \mathbb{R}$ be nonempty and bounded above. Then the supremum of $A$ is unique.
\end{theorem*}

\begin{proof}[Proof of \ref{prop:sup-unique}(1)]
Assume $s$ and $t$ are both suprema of $A$. We will show that $s=t$.

Since $s=\sup A$, $s$ is an upper bound of $A$, and since $t=\sup A$ is the least upper bound of $A$, it follows that
\[
t \le s.
\]

Similarly, since $t=\sup A$, $t$ is an upper bound of $A$, and since $s=\sup A$ is the least upper bound of $A$, it follows that
\[
s \le t.
\]

Therefore,
\[
s \le t \quad \text{and} \quad t \le s.
\]

By antisymmetry of the order on $\mathbb{R}$, we conclude that
\[
s=t.
\]

Hence the supremum of $A$ is unique.
\end{proof}
