\begin{proof}
\Claim Let $(X,d)$ be a metric space. If $x \neq y$ then $d(x,y) > 0$.

\Given A metric space $(X,d)$ and points $x, y \in X$ with $x \neq y$.

\Goal To show $d(x,y) > 0$.

\Strategy You know $d(x,y) \ge 0$ from M1. You know $d(x,y) = 0$ would force
what about $x$ and $y$ from M2? Use the contrapositive.

\begin{remark*}[Proof shape]
Contrapositive of the identity axiom: $d(x,y) = 0 \Rightarrow x = y$,
combined with non-negativity to lift $\ge 0$ to $> 0$.
\end{remark*}
