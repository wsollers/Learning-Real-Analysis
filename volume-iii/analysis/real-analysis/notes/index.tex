\medskip
\noindent\textbf{How we got here.}
Volume~II established the real numbers as a complete ordered field
and proved the axiomatic foundations: field and order axioms,
bounds, suprema, and the completeness axiom.
Real analysis takes these tools and develops the theory of sequences,
series, and their limiting behaviour on $\mathbb{R}$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What this chapter builds.}
We develop rigorous $\varepsilon$-$N$ analysis on $\mathbb{R}$:
convergence of sequences, the algebra of limits, monotone convergence,
Cauchy sequences, subsequences and Bolzano--Weierstrass,
limit superior and inferior, and infinite series with convergence tests.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Where this leads.}
Metric spaces generalise the distance structure of $\mathbb{R}$ to
arbitrary sets. Every theorem here has a metric-space analogue.
Real analysis on $\mathbb{R}$ is the prototype for all of it.

\subsection*{Structural Roadmap}

\begin{center}
\textbf{Sequences $\longrightarrow$ Convergence
$\longrightarrow$ Subsequences $\longrightarrow$ Series
$\longrightarrow$ Asymptotics $\longrightarrow$ Applications}
\end{center}

The axiomatic foundations of $\mathbb{R}$ (field axioms, intervals,
bounds, completeness) live in \textbf{Volume~II, Chapter: Real Numbers}.
The progression here begins where those foundations end:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Sequences and boundedness
  \item Convergence and the algebra of limits
  \item Monotone sequences and the monotone convergence theorem
  \item Cauchy sequences and completeness revisited
  \item Subsequences, Bolzano--Weierstrass, and the subsequence toolkit
  \item Recurrence inequalities and iterative control
  \item Limit superior and limit inferior
  \item Growth and asymptotics
  \item Infinite series and partial sums
  \item Absolute and conditional convergence
  \item Convergence tests (comparison, ratio, root, alternating)
  \item Rearrangements and power series
  \item Applications and worked examples
\end{enumerate}
