\section{Algebraic Structure of \texorpdfstring{$\mathbb{N}$}{N}}

Once the arithmetic and order laws have been proved concretely, the chapter
can finally package what kind of object $\mathbb{N}$ is: additive monoid,
multiplicative monoid, semiring, and the structural limitations that explain
why subtraction is not a total internal operation.

\begin{remark}[Structural endpoint]
This is where the chapter should say plainly what algebraic structure
$\mathbb{N}$ has, instead of reaching for field language that does not fit.
\end{remark}
