\section{Multiplication on \texorpdfstring{$\mathbb{N}$}{N}}

This section should define multiplication recursively and then derive its
basic consequences: multiplication by zero, multiplication by one,
multiplication by successor, distributivity over addition, associativity,
commutativity, and the multiplicative identity.

\begin{remark}[Parallel with addition]
Multiplication should feel like the second major recursive operation on
$\mathbb{N}$, not like an imported black box.
\end{remark}
