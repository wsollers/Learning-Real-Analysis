\section{Powers and Growth}

This section should house exponentiation together with the first major growth
patterns on $\mathbb{N}$: power laws, factorials, binomial coefficients, the
binomial theorem, and basic comparisons among arithmetic, polynomial, and
exponential growth.

\begin{remark}[Why this stays in the chapter]
These topics feel like the natural culmination of arithmetic on $\mathbb{N}$,
not an unrelated enrichment.
\end{remark}
