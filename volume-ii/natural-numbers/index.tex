\medskip
\noindent\textbf{How we got here.}
The Peano axioms in Volume~I (Axiom Systems) constructed $\mathbb{N}$
from scratch and proved all arithmetic properties by induction.
This chapter collects the resulting properties of $\mathbb{N}$ ---
arithmetic, order, well-ordering, and divisibility --- as a
self-contained reference.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What this chapter will build.}
Properties of addition and multiplication, the well-ordering principle,
strong induction, divisibility, primes, and the fundamental theorem
of arithmetic.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Where this leads.}
The integers extend $\mathbb{N}$ by adjoining additive inverses.
Every fact about $\mathbb{N}$ carries forward into $\mathbb{Z}$,
$\mathbb{Q}$, and $\mathbb{R}$.
