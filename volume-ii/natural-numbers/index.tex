\noindent\textbf{How we got here.}
Volume~I built the Peano framework and established that recursive
definitions on $\mathbb{N}$ are legitimate. This chapter turns that logical
foundation into arithmetic.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What this chapter develops.}
We begin from the Peano foundations, define addition and multiplication by
recursion, prove the corresponding algebraic and order laws, isolate the
induction toolkit that powers those proofs, and then package the resulting
structure of $\mathbb{N}$ in a form that can feed directly into $\mathbb{Z}$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Where this leads.}
The natural numbers support counting and recursive arithmetic, but not
internal subtraction. That limitation is exactly what motivates the
construction of the integers.
\end{tcolorbox}
