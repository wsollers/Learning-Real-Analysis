\medskip
\noindent\textbf{How we got here.}
Proof techniques gave us the tools to reason rigorously.
We now apply those tools to build $\mathbb{N}$ from scratch,
starting from five axioms and nothing else.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What this chapter builds.}
We follow Tao's \emph{Analysis I}, Chapter~2.
We lay down the Peano axioms, define addition and multiplication
by recursion, and prove every property of arithmetic that is
normally assumed without justification.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Where this leads.}
The integers $\mathbb{Z}$, rationals $\mathbb{Q}$, and reals $\mathbb{R}$
are all constructed from $\mathbb{N}$ by successively adjoining
inverses and completing. Every algebraic structure built later
inherits the patterns established here.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection*{Structural Roadmap}

The development follows Tao's axiomatic construction of $\mathbb{N}$
from the ground up. The architecture is:

\begin{center}
\textbf{Axioms $\longrightarrow$ Definitions $\longrightarrow$
Lemmas $\longrightarrow$ Propositions $\longrightarrow$ Corollaries}
\end{center}

Nothing is assumed; every fact must be derived from what precedes it.
The available toolkit at each step is exactly what has been proved so far.

The global progression is:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Peano axioms: the logical skeleton of $\mathbb{N}$
  \item Addition: defined recursively, all properties proved
  \item Order: defined via addition, full trichotomy proved
  \item Multiplication: defined recursively, distributive law proved
\end{enumerate}
