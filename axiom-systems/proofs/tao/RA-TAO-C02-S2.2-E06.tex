\textbf{Goal.}
Let $n$ be a natural number, and let $P(m)$ be a property pertaining to the natural
numbers such that whenever $P(m{+}{+})$ is true, then $P(m)$ is true. Suppose that
$P(n)$ is also true. Prove that $P(m)$ is true for all natural numbers $m \le n$;
this is known as the \emph{principle of backwards induction}.
\emph{(Hint: apply induction to the variable $n$.)}
